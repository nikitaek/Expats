## 1. Market Overview — The 2026 Russian Vietnam Boom

Russian tourism to Vietnam has grown at an unprecedented pace since the resumption of charter flights in 2024–2025. The trajectory represents one of the most significant bilateral tourism recoveries in the current decade.

| Year | Russian Arrivals to Vietnam | Growth |
|------|----------------------------|--------|
| 2019 | 646,500 | Previous record |
| 2024 | ~300,000 | Partial recovery [[7]](https://luckywings.ru/blog/otkrytie-granic-vetnama-dlja-turistov-iz-rossii-poslednie-novosti-uslovija-vezda/) |
| 2025 | ~690,000 | +130% YoY [[7]](https://luckywings.ru/blog/otkrytie-granic-vetnama-dlja-turistov-iz-rossii-poslednie-novosti-uslovija-vezda/) |
| 2026 (projected) | 1,000,000+ | Record, +45%+ YoY [[1]](https://www.atorus.ru/article/vetnam-letom-2026-kuda-letyat-rossiyane-i-iz-kakikh-gorodov-est-tury-67793) |

The acceleration in 2026 is driven by three structural factors: the extension of Vietnam's visa-free policy for Russian citizens to **45 days** (from 15 days) [[179]](https://www.myvietnamvisa.com/visa-requirements/russia.html), the launch of direct Da Nang charter programs for the first time, and the significant expansion of regional Russian cities with direct flights to Vietnam.

In April 2026 alone, **137,221 Russian tourists** arrived in Vietnam, a 228.2% increase versus April 2025's 41,806 arrivals [[1]](https://www.atorus.ru/article/vetnam-letom-2026-kuda-letyat-rossiyane-i-iz-kakikh-gorodov-est-tury-67793). The first four months of 2026 produced **504,389 Russian arrivals** to Vietnam, a 203% increase over the same period in 2025 (166,460 arrivals) [[1]](https://www.atorus.ru/article/vetnam-letom-2026-kuda-letyat-rossiyane-i-iz-kakikh-gorodov-est-tury-67793). Vietnam has consequently risen from 11th place (0.5% of total Russian tour sales) to **5th place** (5.5% of total Russian tour sales) in the ranking of destinations for Russian outbound travel [[2]](https://profi.travel/news/64000/details).

**Da Nang specifically** received **142,000+ Russian and CIS tourists** in the first four months of 2026, representing approximately 3.7% of total international arrivals and a **20% increase** over the same period in 2025 [[45]](https://www.thesaigonpost.com/2026/05/why-da-nang-city-is-rapidly-becoming.html) [[44]](https://nomadlawyer.org/da-nang-direct-flights-moscow-russia-tourism-2026). In all of 2025, Da Nang received 171,000 Russian tourists — nearly double its 2024 figure [[51]](https://www.vietnam.vn/en/da-nang-tro-thanh-vung-dat-moi-cua-du-khach-nga-doanh-nghiep-cap-tap-chuan-bi). Russian searches for Da Nang on Yandex increased **260%** in January–February 2026 versus January–February 2025 [[51]](https://www.vietnam.vn/en/da-nang-tro-thanh-vung-dat-moi-cua-du-khach-nga-doanh-nghiep-cap-tap-chuan-bi). Da Nang participated in MITT 2026 (Moscow's 32nd International Travel & Hospitality Exhibition, March 11, 2026) for the first time with its own dedicated stand, conducting approximately **360 B2B meetings** with Russian and CIS tour operators [[43]](https://english.vov.vn/en/travel/vietnam-tourism-promoted-at-mitt-travel-fair-in-moscow-led-by-da-nang-post1275057.vov).

The typical Russian tourist profile for Vietnam is: stays of 6–12 nights at 3–5 star accommodation, high spending levels, strong preference for beach destinations with excursion programs, and growing interest in cultural add-ons including Hoi An [[51]](https://www.vietnam.vn/en/da-nang-tro-thanh-vung-dat-moi-cua-du-khach-nga-doanh-nghiep-cap-tap-chuan-bi) [[200]](https://www.vietnam.vn/en/khach-nga-dang-tim-kiem-dieu-gi-o-viet-nam-ma-da-nang-phu-quoc-cap-tap-chuan-bi-don-tiep).

## 10. Hoi An Selling Strategy — How It Is Positioned in the Russian Market

The research is unambiguous on this point: **Hoi An is not sold as a standalone beach charter product** in any current major Russian tour operator program. Its commercial positioning follows a clear pattern.

**Primary model — Beach + Culture Combo:** Russian tourists purchase 9–11 night packages that combine Da Nang (beach portion, 3 nights) with Hoi An (cultural/quieter beach portion, 6–7 nights). ANEX Tour's "6+3" and "7+3" formats are the industry standard [[101]](https://www.atorus.ru/node/68024) [[9]](https://www.atorus.ru/article/turoperatory-zapuskayut-chartery-na-novyy-kurort-vetnama-65996).

**Secondary model — Multi-city excursion tours:** ICS Travel Group, PAC Group, TurTransVoyage, Planeta Travel, Unicondor (Ukraine), Aviko-Tour (Ukraine), San Wei Tour (Ukraine), and Intourist all include Hoi An as a 1–2 night stop on North-to-South or South-to-North Vietnam excursion routes [[174]](https://www.icstrvl.ru/city/spb/news/Vetnam-ekskursionnie-turi/index.html) [[175]](https://www.icstrvl.ru/city/spb/news/Vetnam-assortiment-turov/index.html) [[54]](https://www.tourtrans.ru/actual/vietnam) [[57]](https://plantravel.ru/countries/vietnam/) [[71]](https://unicondor.com.ua/map/asia/vetnam.html).

**Demand seasonality:** The dry season for Da Nang and Hoi An runs February–August [[38]](https://intourist.ru/info/vetnam) [[9]](https://www.atorus.ru/article/turoperatory-zapuskayut-chartery-na-novyy-kurort-vetnama-65996), which aligns with Russia's summer outbound season. Summer charter programs (April–October 2026) therefore have ideal climatic alignment. October–January brings the rainy season to Central Vietnam, which redirects Russian charter programs toward Nha Trang, Phu Quoc, and Phan Thiet.

**Excursion marketing:** Russian tourists flying into Da Nang are marketed the following excursion program: Hoi An Ancient Town, Ba Na Hills, Son Tra Peninsula, Marble Mountains, Golden Bridge [[48]](https://www.vietnam.vn/en/khach-nga-tro-lai-da-nang-72-000-nguoi-duoc-don-tren-cac-chuyen-bay-thue-bao) [[51]](https://www.vietnam.vn/en/da-nang-tro-thanh-vung-dat-moi-cua-du-khach-nga-doanh-nghiep-cap-tap-chuan-bi). Hoi An is consistently described in Russian-language materials as a mandatory cultural add-on and has been explicitly described by VietOne Travel DMC as "consistently one of the most popular destinations with Russian travellers" [[171]](https://vietone.travel/russian-travellers-in-vietnam/).

**Key pricing points for planning:** Da Nang–Hoi An combo 7 nights from \$125,337 RUB per 2 adults from Moscow (Tourvisor June 2026 minimum) [[6]](https://tourvisor.ru/tours/vietnam/moskva). TEZ Tour lists Hoi An 5-star packages from \$80,790 RUB (7 nights breakfast, Hoiana Residences, May 2026) to \$697,875 RUB (8 nights half-board, Four Seasons, January 2027) [[15]](https://www.tez-tour.travel/vietnam/).
